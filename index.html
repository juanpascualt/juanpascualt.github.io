Education · Crime · Public Economics

From Detention to Prevention: Firm-Based Vocational Education and Crime

(with Daniel Montolio, Jenifer Ruiz Valenzuela )

This paper examines the impact of an innovative firm-based vocational training program (DVT) on youth crime rates in Catalonia. Exploiting the staggered rollout across municipalities, we find that the program significantly reduces criminal activity among young individuals, particularly property crimes and violent offenses.

Reshuffling the Desks? Affirmative Action, Segregation, and Educational Attainment

(with Clemens Oberhuemer, Sergi Quintana )

Barcelona's 2019 admission quotas reserve seats for socioeconomically vulnerable students in each public and charter school. We estimate the effects of this policy on school segregation and student outcomes using administrative data and a difference-in-differences strategy.
